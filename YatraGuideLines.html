Annual Spiritual Yatra 3.0 2025

Vrindavan & Nathdwara Pilgrimage

27 Sep - 5 Oct 2025 Limited Seats Available
Yatra Guidelines

Sacred Journey to Divine Abodes

Embark on this transformative spiritual journey to the holy lands of Vrindavan and Nathdwara, following in the footsteps of Lord Krishna and experiencing the divine presence in these sacred places.

Spiritual Elevation

Experience spiritual elevation on the divine Yatra — a journey of devotion, purification, and divine connection in sacred association.

Holy Darshans

Visit ancient temples and have darshans of revered deities including Banke Bihari, ISKCON Krishna Balaram temple, and Shrinathji.

Sacred Sites

Explore important pilgrimage sites like Vrindavan, Govardhan, Radha Kund, Barsana, Nandgaon & Nathdwara

Yatra Guidelines

To ensure a smooth and spiritually uplifting experience for all participants, please follow these guidelines carefully.

General Guidelines

  • All participants must register their names through online registration and emergency contact details.
  • Maintain punctuality for all scheduled activities and departures.
  • Respect local customs and temple regulations at all holy sites.
  • Smoking, alcohol, and non-vegetarian food are strictly prohibited.
  • The yatra schedule is subject to change based on weather and local conditions.
  • Kindly check the official WhatsApp group for important updates, instructions, and schedule changes during the yatra.

Code of Conduct

  • Maintaining a spiritual atmosphere by engaging in kirtan and japa is encouraged.
  • Dress modestly - women are advised to wear saris or salwar kameez or Indian attire, men are advised to wear dhotis or pants with kurta.
  • Photography may be restricted at certain temples - always ask permission.
  • Avoid political or controversial discussions during the yatra.
  • Respect fellow pilgrims and maintain harmony in the group.
  • Follow instructions from yatra coordinators at all times.

Packing List (advised)

Essential Items (advised)

  • Valid photo ID (Aadhar/DL)
  • Personal medications
  • Water bottle

Clothing (advised)

  • Traditional Indian attire
  • Shawl or light jacket/clothes (weather in October is usually pleasant and not very cold.)

Spiritual Items (advised)

  • Bhagavad-gita or other scriptures
  • Japa mala and bead bag
  • Notebook for recording realizations

Health & Safety

  • Inform coordinators of any medical conditions.
  • Carry personal medications with prescriptions, if essential.
  • Emergency contact numbers will be provided.
